Intro

In 2018, I was deployed to the USS Green Bay as a rider. This was quite an experience because on this trip I not only met my boyfriend, but I also got to become a shellback via the infamous 'shellback ceremony' and coast over to beautiful Cairns, Australia to do some snorkeling on the Great Barrier Reef!

What the Heck is a Shellback Ceremony?

Becoming a shellback is a navy tradition that I find Extremely gratuitous and unnecessary. It involves a series of degrading tasks that wogs do to prove their worth as shellbacks when a ship crosses the equator. I try to not give weight to the title of 'shellback,' which only validates these types of traditions... nevertheless, in Navy circles, it is possible to milk some benefits in social situations that you wouldn't normally have access to, so I do cash in on being a shellback from time to time. In response to the question, 'what the heck is a shellback ceremony?' my answer is simply: some bs made up so crusty, salty sailors can feel superior to land lubbers.

Great Barrer Reef

Snorkeling on the Great Barrier Reef was the cool part of this deployment. Unfortunately, however, due to climate change, there isn't really much left of the GBR to enjoy. The parts we did see looked like a forest fire had gone through underwater with how bleached and dead the coral was. Still, I feel stoked to have gotten to see some parts of it before it completely disappears.
